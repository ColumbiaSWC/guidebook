    Information for Helpers

    What is a helper?

    At Foundations for Research Computing workshops, intensives, and bootcamps, a helper or helpers will circulate in the room to resolve technical issues and answer questions from students. The helper has a critical role in making sure training events go smoothly, as the instructor will typically have limited time and attention for individual students while teaching.

    Helper Info

    Green and Red Sticky Note System

    If you haven't been exposed to it, the Software Carpentry system uses green and red sticky notes to signal instructors and helpers. When an instructor assigns a task or an exercise, the green sticky is placed on the top of the computer screen to show that the student has completed it. When a student puts up a red sticky, that shows they're in need of assistance. There should be stickies (and extra power strips) in the rooms when the instructors start in the morning, but come find Patrick if you need more.

    Be Active and Visible

    As a helper, your job is to circulate actively in the room and assist students when they need it. Being visible as the helper is an important part of the job, as students will hesitate to put up a red sticky if they think a helper isn't around. So when not helping a student, try to stay somewhat visible and present to the students in the room.

    Watch Out For Cables!

    While helping, you'll be moving around a lot, and will be at high risk of tripping over cables and power strips. Extension cords and strips should have been all taped down, but if it looks like I've missed a spot let me know and we'll remedy the situation.

    Don't Get Distracted

    If possible, try to finish helping one student before jumping to another. Though it can be tempting to triage student problems, students can feel abandoned if you jump away to another problem before their issue is resolved.
